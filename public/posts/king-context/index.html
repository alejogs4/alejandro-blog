Context is (was always) the king

Jan 10, 2026

Some years ago one of the tech leads I have respected the most on my career said something that from that moment resulted in a mantra for me:

For bad software we already have Excel

With this he didn’t mean Excel was a bad software, it is and it continues being one of the most incredible pieces of software ever written however most of what we build even for serious projects is not even close to this level of refinenment, uselfulness and robustness but why this happens? why our industry even with the most potent tools ever conceived it seems to always fail to build software products that even just justify its own existance? let’s talk about communication.

Broken telephone game: our ability to say what we mean

As children in school and as adults in corporate trainings we don’t want to attend you might have played the broken telephone game, in this game n group of persons try to pass a message from the first person to the last person trying to keep as much as possible the integrity of the first message, the result almost always is that the message get’s at some level changed from the original message, depending of how much this message was changed this is more serious or it is simply a small inconvenience and this message changing it is because humans are bad at saying literally what other people said to them so using their our language, vocabulary and ways of expressing we tend to replicate what we heard however in this human-to-human translation some original meaning it is lost so when multiply across several chain of people exist a high probability than the original message it is not transmited as was originall intended and for our bad luck this happens not only in day to day communication, this happens in our jobs where the effectiveness and clarity of our words it is a big factor in the success of software products we build.

Your context == Your understanding

When starting in a new job or role there is a time where your managers doesn’t expect you to perform at the level your position would normally require, why? because every organization despite working on same set of technologies, programming languages and even processes it has its own business rules, specific domain knowledge and team dynamics, this is your context, the degree in which you understand the business you are working on, the jargon or the ubiquituos language used by the product and technical team, without understanding this no matter how good programmer you are you won’t produce the work you are asked to do at least not in a good level quality because our job as engineers at some point stops being coding or knowing the fanciest tools, our job is literally understand from who we are working, what makes the money, what are the constraints and business invariants and beiang able to techinically capture this knowledge we have gathered and generated. Of course this has been always the point, programmers in the 60s had to know about physics and math to put the man on the moon just as today we have to know about healthcare and schedulling for a hospital system however with the popularizing on the last few months of agentic coding tools this came to the spotlight, more and more the job of a programmer is less seen as just coding, the industry due to the overflow of money and demand allow programmers just care about coding and business knowledge was almost a nice to have that more senior positions only had to focus but now that mere code writing cost has decreased our understanding is more important than ever.
